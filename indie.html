Meera's Music

I love indie. Indie is a very broad genre, and while I do enjoy many of its different subgenres, my favorite is probably indie rock.

Death Cab For Cutie

Death Cab for Cutie

My favorite indie band is Death Cab for Cutie. I've been a fan of theirs for as long as I can remember, and I was fortunate enough to be able to see them live in concert recently. The lead singer, Ben Gibbard, is also the band's songwriter, and the lyricism seen in many of Death Cab's songs draws greatly from his experiences. As such, the band's music feels very genuine and almost intimate.

Fun fact: it is actually impossible for me to choose a favorite album from their discography; there's something I love about each one. Click on an album cover to learn more.

Something About Airplanes(1998)

This is Death Cab's first album. It has a very raw, recorded-in-the-basement sound.

Something About Airplanes(1998)

We Have The Facts And We're Voting Yes (2000)

This is Death Cab's second album. It really just sounds like a continuation of Something About Airplanes.

We Have The Facts And We're Voting Yes (2000)

The Photo Album (2001)

Their third album is when they start to really get their sound. This album's biggest song is "A Movie Script Ending".

The Photo Album (2001)

Transatlanticism (2003)

This is their first big success. Drawing on themes of long distance and separation, this album has also become a fan-favorite. The most well-known song on this album is probably the title track, for its long, repeated bridge.

Transatlanticism (2003)

Plans(2005)

Their fifth album is the most iconic. Featuring songs like "I Will Follow You Into The Dark" and "What Sarah Said", Plans is centered around the theme of loss.

Plans(2005)

Narrow Stairs (2008)

This album is interesting because the songs all continue into each other, allowing for a very seamless listen of the entire album. This is one of the most existential albums Death Cab has released.

Narrow Stairs (2008)

Codes & Keys (2011)

Here, Death Cab experiments with synths, creating a very different sound from their previous albums. Codes & Keys is also the band's happiest album, as its songs are based largely off of Gibbard's marriage to actress Zooey Deshanel.

Codes & Keys (2011)

Kintsugi (2015)

Kintsugi is the Japanese art of repairing broken pottery with gold. Based off of Gibbard's divorce from Deshanel, this album is about the aftermath of a relationship and the resulting personal growth. The cover is a shredded photograph.

Kintsugi (2015)

Thank You For Today (2018)

This is Death Cab's most mainstream pop record to date. The band drifts away from its traditional sound again. This album has themes of growing older, but in my opinion it isn't well-executed.

Thank You For Today (2018)

Asphalt Meadows (2022)

This is their tenth album. The focus here is that the world changes as we grow older, even if we stay the same. The band modernizes their iconic sound but stays true to their roots, and pays homage to the people who helped them succeed.

Asphalt Meadows (2022)
