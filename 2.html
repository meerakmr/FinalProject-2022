Meera's Music

I love indie. My favorite indie band is Death Cab for Cutie. I've been a fan of theirs for as long as I can remember, and I was fortunate enough to be able to see them live in concert recently. The lead singer, Ben Gibbard, is also the band's songwriter, and the lyricism seen in many of Death Cab's songs draws greatly from his experiences. As such, the band's music feels very genuine and almost intimate.

Fun fact: it is actually impossible for me to choose a favorite album from their discography; there's something I love about each one. Click on an album cover to learn more.

Something About Airplanes.
This is Death Cab's first album. It has a very raw, recorded-in-the-basement sound.

We Have The Facts And We're Voting Yes
This is Death Cab's second album. It really just sounds like a continuation of Airplanes.

The Photo Album
Their third album is when they start to really get their sound. This album's biggest song is "A Movie Script Ending".

Transatlanticism
This is their first big success. Drawing on themes of long distance and separation, this album has also become a fan-favorite. The most well-known song on this album is probably the title track, for its long, repeated bridge.

Plans
Their fifth album is the most iconic. Featuring songs like "I Will Follow You Into The Dark" and "What Sarah Said", Plans is centered around the theme of loss.

Narrow Stairs
This album is interesting because the songs all continue into each other, allowing for a very seamless listen of the entire album. This is one of the most existential albums Death Cab has released, and it features an 8-minute song titled "I Will Possess Your Heart". It's probably my least favorite song of theirs.

Codes & Keys
In this album, Death Cab experiments with synths, creating a very different sound from their previous albums. I personally love this, although many fans do not. Codes & Keys is also the band's happiest album, as its songs are based largely off of Gibbard's (short-lived) marriage to actress Zooey Deshanel.

Kintsugi
Kintsugi is the Japanese art of repairing broken pottery with gold. Based off of Gibbard's divorce from Deshanel, this album is about the aftermath of a relationship and the resulting growth that occurs. The album cover is an old photograph that was shredded and warped.

Thank You For Today
This is Death Cab's most mainstream pop record to date. The band drifts away from its traditional sound again (see: Codes & Keys). This album has themes of growing older, but in my opinion these themes aren't well-executed.

Asphalt Meadows
This is their tenth and most recent studio album, the tour for which I got to attend. The theme here is the way in which the world changes as we grow older, even if we stay the same. They modernize their iconic sound in a way that stays true to their roots, and pay homage to the people who helped them get to where they are now.
