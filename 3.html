Meera's Music

I love metal. I'm not sure what exactly drew me to the genre, but nonetheless, metal has been one of my most-listened-to genres for the past few years. My two favorite metal bands are Motionless in White and Ice Nine Kills, both of whom I got the chance to see in concert. They're both considered to be under the heavy metal umbrella, as part of the subgenre of metal called metalcore, which in my experience involves more clean vocals and cool guitar riffs/breakdowns.


Motionless in White is a classic metalcore band. Over the course of their discography, they've experimented quite a bit in order to refine their sound. Click on an album cover to learn more about it.

Creatures
Their first album is the fan-favorite. Motionless has even released merch shirts with the text "Motionless In White Hasn't Been Good Since Creatures" on the front.

Infamous
Their second album is widely regarded as their worst. For this album, they went out of the standard metal mold, but they weren't met with much positivity. It did, however, inspire them to keep experimenting.

Reincarnate
This album is my favorite. Here, Motionless incorporated many facets of industrial metal (eg. synths, repeated riffs) and various aspects of gothic metal (which is exactly what it sounds like).

Graveyard Shift
Their fourth album sees the band exploring the sounds they cultivated in Reincarnate. They pay homage to their hometown in "570" and the album revolves around themes of how hard work and dedication are necessary for success.

Disguise
This is the album that introduced me to the band, so it is one of my favorites. The brighter colors in the cover are indicative of the high energy levels seen in the songs on this album. They also dig deeper into their heavy metal sound here.

Scoring the End of the World
This album takes a very dramatic turn, as the theme is centered around the issues humans have caused to the planet (eg. global warming) as well as each other (eg. greed in capitalism). The fan-favorite off of this album is "Slaughterhouse".




Ice Nine Kills describes themselves as a "theatricore" band, and I completely agree. Their early work was a little too scene for me, but when "The Silver Scream" came out, I think they really came into their own, commercially and artistically. Their focus over time has shifted towards horror in pop culture, with songs based off of books like "Carrie" and movies like "American Psycho". Click on an album cover to learn more about it.

Every Trick in the Book
This album marks the beginning of INK's pop culture horror era. The album is filled with horror book references, calling on books like "Dracula", "Animal Farm", and "Carrie".

The Silver Scream
This album was what made INK special. It was the first to achieve mass popularity in the metal community, and the first I had heard of them. Songs here reference horror movies like "IT", "Jaws", "Saw", and "Friday the 13th" (my favorite).

The Silver Scream 2: Welcome to Horrorwood
Their most recent album builds off of the momentum gained from The Silver Scream. They continue basing songs off of movies, this time referencing "Hostel", "Psycho", and "Child's Play", among others.
